02 — Typography

Type system

Two typefaces. Fraunces (serif) gives headlines and pull-quotes a timeless, editorial warmth that suits storytelling. Inter (sans) handles body copy and UI for clean legibility. The script wordmark lives only in the logo — never set live text in a script font.

03 — Logo

The wordmark

The vintage athletic script — "Delta Sigs" anchored by the NIU banner. It's the heart of the brand; protect it. Use the white-stroke version over photos and green; the plain version on white and cream.

On white
On Carnation White
On Nile Green (white stroke)
On photo (darkened + white stroke)

Clear space & minimum size

Keep clear space equal to the height of the NIU banner on all four sides. Minimum digital width ≈ 160px so the NIU lettering stays legible.

↕ banner-height

Do

  • Use the white-stroke variant on photos & green.
  • Give it room — respect the clear space.
  • Keep it on calm, high-contrast areas.

Don't

  • Recolor, add gradients, shadows or 3D.
  • Stretch, skew, or rotate the mark.
  • Place on busy, low-contrast photos.
04 — Voice & Tone

How we sound

Warm, plain-spoken, and brotherhood-first. We're not marketing — we're catching up with old friends. Nostalgic but never stiff; proud but humble.

Say this
  • "Where are they now? Hear it from the brothers themselves."
  • "Forty years later, the stories are still good."
  • "Pull up a chair — let's catch up."
Not this
  • "Leverage our exclusive alumni content platform."
  • "Sign up now to unlock premium member assets."
  • "Engage with our digital brotherhood ecosystem."
Principles
  • Personal — first names, real memories.
  • Brief — short sentences, no jargon.
  • Inviting — every line nudges a reconnect.
05 — Imagery

Photography

Real, candid chapter moments — homecoming, gatherings, workouts, reunions. Favor warmth and faces over staged shots. A Nile-green duotone is available for hero overlays and section dividers.

06 — Iconography

Icons

Flat, minimalist line icons (Lucide style) — 24×24, 2px stroke, drawn with currentColor so they pick up brand colors. No emoji as functional icons.
